Life Reminders Support

Smart Life Deadline Tracker · iPhone · iPad · Mac · Apple Watch · Android

System Requirements

  • iOS iPhone, iPad, or Mac running iOS / iPadOS / macOS 17 or later. The Apple Watch companion requires watchOS 10 or later.
  • Android Android 8.0 (Oreo, API 26) or later — covers ~97% of active devices. Voice input runs fully on-device on Android 12+ (API 31); on Android 8–11, transcription may fall back to Google's speech services depending on device.

If the app won't install or a feature appears missing, please check your OS version first — most install issues are resolved by updating to the latest supported release.

Getting Started

Adding Reminders

  • iPhone iPad Tap the blue mic button (voice) or keyboard button (manual)
  • Mac Click the mic icon in the toolbar (Cmd+R) or + icon (Cmd+N)
  • Android Tap the blue mic button (voice) or the smaller + button (manual). Both float at the bottom-right of the Reminders screen.
  • Apple Watch View and complete reminders created on other devices

Voice example: "My mom's birthday is on March 13" — the app understands category, person, and date automatically.

Three Views

  • Dashboard — reminders grouped by urgency (Overdue / Today / This Week / Upcoming / Later)
  • Timeline — reminders grouped by time horizon (week / month / 3 months / 6 months / year / later)
  • Calendar — Week, Month, or Year grid with colored dots on dates with reminders. Tap a date to filter the list below.

iPhone Swipe left/right on the Reminders tab to switch views.

iPad Mac Use the sidebar to navigate between views.

Android Use the top tab row inside the Reminders bottom-nav destination to switch views.

Completing Reminders

iPhone iPad Swipe right on any reminder to mark it done.

Mac Right-click a reminder → Complete, or press Cmd+Return in detail view.

Android Tap any reminder card → "Mark Done & Advance" (recurring) or "Mark Complete" (one-time) in the detail sheet.

Apple Watch Tap the reminder → Done button.

Recurring reminders (birthdays, taxes, passport renewals) automatically schedule the next occurrence — you don't need to re-create them.

Notifications

iOS & Android I'm not getting notifications

Open Life Reminders → Settings → Notifications row. It will show "Enabled" or "Disabled — tap to enable."

  • If it says Disabled, tap the row. On Android 13+ this triggers the system permission prompt; on older platforms it opens the system app notification settings.
  • If it says Enabled but you still aren't getting alerts, see the battery optimization section below.

Android Battery optimization is killing my reminders

Some Android manufacturers (Samsung, Xiaomi, OnePlus, Huawei) aggressively kill background apps to save battery, which prevents AlarmManager from firing reminders on time. Add Life Reminders to the battery optimization exclusion list:

  • Samsung: Settings → Apps → Life Reminders → Battery → set to Unrestricted
  • Xiaomi / MIUI: Settings → Apps → Manage apps → Life Reminders → Battery saver → No restrictions, plus Autostart → On
  • OnePlus / OxygenOS: Settings → Apps → Life Reminders → Battery → Battery optimization → Don't optimize
  • Pixel / stock Android: usually works without intervention

Plans & Purchases

How do I restore my Pro purchase?

iOS Settings → Your Plan → "Restore Purchases." Use the same Apple ID as the original purchase.

Android Settings → Plan → "Restore previous purchase." Use the same Google account that bought it.

Pro is Family Sharing compatible on both platforms — family members on the same Apple Family or Google Play Family can install Life Reminders for free and they'll automatically inherit Pro on their own devices.

How do I remove ads?

Settings → Plan → "Upgrade to Pro" ($4.99 one-time). Removes ads, unlocks unlimited reminders. On iOS, also enables iCloud sync across all your Apple devices.

Sync & Multi-Device

iOS Pro How does iCloud sync work?

Life Reminders Pro on iOS uses iCloud to sync your reminders across iPhone, iPad, Mac, and Apple Watch. Add a reminder on your iPhone, complete it on your Mac, check it on your Watch — all in real-time. Each device schedules its own local notifications, so you'll get alerts wherever you are.

Android Can I sync between my Android phone and tablet?

Not yet. Sync between Android devices and the Android ↔ iOS bridge are both planned for version 1.1 via an optional Google Drive integration. For now, your Android reminders live only on the device where you created them. On iOS, sync is available for Apple Pro users.

iOS & Android I have an iPhone AND an Android phone — can I use both?

You can install Life Reminders on both, but until v1.1 ships cross-platform sync, you'll need to manage reminders separately on each device. Both apps have the same 60+ categories and the same voice input, so the experience is consistent — only the data is per-device for now.

Voice Input

Voice isn't recognizing my speech

  • iOS & Android Make sure microphone permission is granted in your phone's Settings → Apps → Life Reminders → Permissions → Microphone.
  • Speak after you tap the mic button, not before — recording only starts after the button bounces.
  • If the parsed date is wrong, you can edit it before tapping Save — voice parsing is a starting point, not the final answer.

Does voice need internet?

iOS No. Voice runs entirely on-device via Apple's SFSpeechRecognizer.

Android On Android 12 and newer (Pixel, modern Samsung, Xiaomi, etc.), voice runs entirely on-device — your audio never leaves your phone. On Android 8–11, the app requests offline-preferred recognition but the device may fall back to Google's cloud speech service if the on-device model isn't available.

Region-Specific Categories

How do I see categories like PAN Card / MOT Test / TÜV?

Life Reminders auto-detects your storefront region from your phone's locale settings. To switch regions:

iOS Settings → General → Language & Region → Region → pick your target country.

Android Settings → System → Languages & input → Languages → add a language for your target region (e.g., English (India)) and drag it to the top.

Region-specific categories appear automatically: PAN Card / Aadhaar / PUC for India, MOT / TV Licence for the UK, TÜV for Germany, Contrôle Technique / Ramonage for France, COE for Singapore, Smog Check / FSA Funds for the US.

What's not yet supported

  • Sync between Android devices — coming in v1.1
  • Wear OS companion app — Apple Watch ships with v1; Wear OS is on the v2 roadmap
  • Themed icons for Material You (Android 13+) — the launcher icon shows in full color, not the wallpaper-tinted monochrome variant. Polish item for v1.1.
  • Shared ads ID across the household — Family Sharing of the Pro purchase works on both platforms, but the ad-supported tier is per-device.
